Goal

Get more enterprise buyers to book a demo from the pricing page.

Recommendation

Swap the hero marketing band for a live trust dashboard with an engineer-led demo CTA — because enterprise buyers convert on demonstrated reliability, and the control offers zero verifiable evidence above the fold.

The “why” behind the recommendations

Live Trust Dashboard Recommended option

  • What: Live uptime + compliance dashboard replaces the marketing band.
  • Why: Demonstrated reliability converts platform engineers; claims don't.
Agent prompt

Hypothesis: replacing the hero marketing band with a live trust dashboard should lift enterprise demo requests because demonstrated reliability converts buyers that claims cannot. Evidence: whitespace — 0 of 14 in-category references render live metrics on pricing (directional, no measured lift); mechanism proof from quantified-outcome case-study pages. Skip if: status-page numbers are unflattering or too young to be credible. Build: slim plan rail at top; full-width dark band with 90-day uptime sparkline, incident strip, downloadable SOC 2/ISO artifacts, customer logos with workload stats; primary CTA "Book a demo with an infrastructure engineer".

Procurement-Ready Plan Column

  • What: SLA, price anchor, and badges printed inside the Enterprise column.
  • Why: Removes every procurement blocker without leaving the plan grid.
Agent prompt

Hypothesis: printing the SLA number, a "$2k/mo starting" anchor, and compliance badges inside the Enterprise column should lift inquiries because procurement blockers are answered in place. Evidence: 2 of 2 captured direct competitors print the SLA in-column; 9 of 14 references run the column convention. Skip if: legal will not commit to a public SLA number. Build: keep the grid; rework the Enterprise column with SLA row, anchor price, badge strip, embedded scheduler under "Book a demo".

Self-Serve Procurement Room

  • What: One email field unlocks a data room with SOC 2, DPA, sample invoice.
  • Why: Procurement needs artifacts, not adjectives or sales calls.
Agent prompt

Hypothesis: replacing "Contact sales" with a self-serve data room (order form with price anchor, SOC 2 report, DPA, sample invoice, migration guide) behind one email field should produce more qualified leads because procurement evaluates artifacts. Evidence: mechanism-only — single source, outside this category; 0 of 14 publish procurement artifacts on pricing. Skip if: sales insists on qualifying every enterprise lead by phone first. Build: replace the Enterprise CTA with "Open the procurement room"; room lists downloadable artifacts plus an instant read-only sandbox link.
