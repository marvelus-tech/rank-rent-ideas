# Austin Land Flip Workflow — MarvelUs Land

## Overview

This skill executes the **builder-first land wholesaling pipeline** for the Austin, Texas metropolitan area. Austin is a **premium infill market** — not a tax-delinquent wholesale market. The strategy focuses on **relationship-driven deal flow** with builders who need build-ready infill lots.

**Key Insight:** Austin's 78757 ZIP (Allandale/Brentwood/Crestview) has lots listed at $550K-$800K+ for 0.17-0.33 acres. Assignment fees here are $25K-$50K+, not $10K.

## The "Austin Donut" Strategy

Instead of just core Austin (expensive), target the **ring around Austin** where:
- Land is cheaper ($50K-$200K vs $500K+)
- Builders are desperate for lots
- Infrastructure is expanding rapidly
- Assignment fees are $15K-$30K (vs $50K+ in core)

**Key Data Points:**
- HOME Phase 2 (Aug 2024): Minimum lot size dropped from 5,750 sq ft to **1,800 sq ft** — theoretically allows 4 homes on a typical 8,000 sq ft lot
- Austin area 2024 total housing starts: **16,802** (up 6% from 15,856 in 2023)
- All-time high: 2021 with 26,704 starts
- Source: Zonda Advisory

## Step 2: Find Vacant Land Opportunities

### Method A: MLS & Public Listings (Fastest)

**Platforms:**
- Zillow — zillow.com/austin-tx/land
- Realtor.com — realtor.com/land-for-sale
- LoopNet — loopnet.com (commercial lots)
- LandSearch — landsearch.com (rural lots)
- Homes.com — homes.com (residential lots)

**Search Filters:**
- Property type: Land/Lot
- Price: $50K-$500K (adjust by market tier)
- Lot size: 0.15+ acres
- Days on market: 30+ (motivated sellers)
- Keywords: "teardown," "value in land," "build your dream"

### Method B: Off-Market & Direct Mail

**Target Lists:**
1. **Out-of-state owners** — TCAD records, absentee landlords
2. **Inherited properties** — probate court records
3. **Long-term holders** — owned 10+ years, high equity
4. **Tax-delinquent** — Travis County Tax Office (limited in Austin proper)

**Direct Mail Script:**
```
"Dear [Owner Name],

I'm a local land buyer interested in purchasing your property at 
[Address]. I'm not a realtor — I'm a direct buyer who can close 
quickly with cash.

If you're interested in selling, I'd love to make you a fair offer. 
Please call me at [Phone] or reply to this letter.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
MarvelUs Land"
```

### Method C: Driving for Dollars (Infill)

1. Drive target ZIPs (78757, 78756, 78759, 78731)
2. Look for:
   - Vacant lots between houses
   - Overgrown/abandoned properties
   - "For Sale By Owner" signs
   - Properties with deteriorating structures
3. Record addresses, take photos
4. Research ownership via TCAD (travis.prodigycad.com)

### Method D: Regrid Research (Free Tier)

1. Create free account at regrid.com (25 lookups/day)
2. Filter Travis County:
   - Land Use = "Vacant" or "Residential Vacant"
   - Acreage: 0.15+
   - No building footprint
3. Cross-reference with TCAD for ownership
4. Prioritize: individual owners, owned 5+ years

## Step 3: Underwriting Checklist (7-Point Screen)

For each parcel, evaluate:

| # | Criteria | Pass/Fail | Notes |
|---|----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | **Infill vs Raw** | Infill preferred | Houses on both sides = build-ready |
| 2 | **Utilities** | Must have | Water/sewer at street, electric poles |
| 3 | **Terrain** | Flat/gentle slope | Sloped = expensive grading |
| 4 | **Road Access** | Paved frontage | Unpaved = deal killer for most builders |
| 5 | **Flood Zone** | 500-yr OK, 100-yr BAD | Check FEMA flood maps |
| 6 | **Zoning** | Residential allowed | SF-1, SF-2, SF-3, MF (Austin) |
| 7 | **Tree Ordinance** | Check protected trees | Austin has strict tree mitigation |

### Austin-Specific Considerations

- **HOME Amendments (2023-2024):** SF lots can now allow up to 3 units
- **Minimum lot size:** Reduced to 1,800 sq ft in some neighborhoods
- **Tree mitigation:** Protected/heritage trees = $$$ to remove/replace
- **Impact fees:** ~$7,700 per service unit for water/wastewater
- **MUDs:** Check if property is in Municipal Utility District

## Step 4: Comp & Price Analysis

### Comparable Sales (Zillow)
- Filter: SOLD lots, last 6 months
- Same ZIP or adjacent
- Similar lot size (+/- 20%)
- Similar characteristics (infill, utilities, zoning)

### Pricing Formula
```
Your Maximum Offer = (Builder's Max Price × 0.75) - Assignment Fee

Where:
- Builder's Max Price = What they'll pay (from Step 1)
- 0.75 = 25% margin for you + negotiation room
- Assignment Fee = Your profit ($25K-$50K for Austin infill)
```

### Example (78757 Infill):
- Builder max: $600K
- Your offer to seller: $600K × 0.75 = $450K
- Assignment fee: $150K
- **Verify:** Comps support $600K sale price

## Step 5: Contract & Close

### Purchase & Sale Agreement (PSA)
- Use Texas-specific contract
- Include feasibility/inspection period (14-21 days)
- Earnest money: $100-$500 (keep it low)
- "And/or assigns" clause

### Assignment Agreement
- Assign PSA rights to builder buyer
- Assignment fee disclosed or double-close
- Builder pays assignment fee at closing

### Title Company (Investor-Friendly)
Must confirm:
- "Do you work with wholesalers?" → Yes
- "Do you handle assignment of contracts?" → Yes
- "Do you do double closings?" → Yes (bonus)

**Austin Title Companies:**
- Independence Title (investor-friendly)
- Austin Title
- Trinity Title of Texas
