# Moo Hong Phuket (หมูฮ้องภูเก็ต)

**Source:** Stay Home Phuket (YouTube)  
**Chef:** Mom Yupin  
**Dish:** Phuket-style braised pork belly — a traditional recipe over 100 years old  
**Date Saved:** 2026-06-21

---

## 📝 Description

Moo Hong is a classic Phuket dish of braised pork belly, slow-cooked until the meat is tender and coated in a rich, glossy, dark sauce. The key is low, slow heat and patience — the longer it stews, the more delicious it becomes.

---

## 🛒 Ingredients

### Main Ingredient
- **Pork belly** — choose pieces with red meat and a little fat (not too fatty or it becomes greasy)

### Aromatics & Spices
- **Garlic** — whole cloves, peeled (used whole for fragrance)
- **Black peppercorns** — crushed/pounded (not too fine)
- **Cinnamon stick**
- **Star anise**
- **Cardamom**

### Seasonings
- **Black soy sauce** — *Car brand* (ตรารถ) — Phuket traditional black soy sauce for color and unique aroma
- **White soy sauce** — *Angel brand* (ตรานางฟ้า) — Phuket traditional white soy sauce for fragrance
- **Salt** — clear salt, used moderately (Moo Hong is not very salty)
- **Coconut sugar** — adds a little sweetness, use moderately

---

## 👨‍🍳 Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the Pork
1. Cut pork belly into large pieces (chef recommends slightly large chunks — they cook down beautifully)

### Step 2: Prepare the Marinade
2. Pound black peppercorns until coarsely crushed
3. Crush garlic (whole peeled cloves)
4. Add crushed garlic and pepper to the pork
5. Add black soy sauce — **generous amount** (this gives the signature Phuket color and scent)
6. Add white soy sauce (Angel brand)
7. Season with salt — moderately
8. Add coconut sugar — moderately
9. Add whole spices: cinnamon, star anise, cardamom
10. **Mix everything thoroughly**
11. **Marinate for 30 minutes** before cooking

### Step 3: Braise the Pork
12. Transfer marinated pork to a pot
13. **Do NOT add water yet**
14. Start boiling over **low heat** — the slower, the better
15. As it cooks, the pork will release its own juices and fat
16. **Stir occasionally** — lift from the bottom to prevent sticking
17. When the liquid starts to dry, **add a little boiling water** — just enough to prevent burning
18. Continue simmering on **very low heat** (110-120°C / 230-250°F)
19. The pork will tighten and release oil — this is normal
20. Keep monitoring: if water dries, add **small amounts** of water
21. **Do NOT add too much water** — it dilutes the richness

### Step 4: Slow Cook to Perfection
22. **Minimum:** 1 hour (taste will be absorbed, pork will be cooked)
23. **Ideal:** 5-6 hours for truly soft, tender, glossy pork
24. The pork will become shiny and beautiful as the fat renders
25. **Taste test:** Check if the flavor has penetrated the meat
26. When done, the sauce should be thick, dark, and coating the pork

### Step 5: Serve
27. Transfer to a bowl
28. The finished dish should have a **rich, glossy, dark color**
29. Serve with steamed rice

---

## 💡 Chef's Tips (from Mom Yupin)

- **Pork selection:** Choose belly with more red meat than fat — too much fat makes it greasy
- **Cut size:** Cut slightly large pieces — they shrink and become tender
- **Heat control:** Low and slow is key. High heat toughens the meat
- **Water discipline:** Add water little by little. Too much water = bland, unattractive pork
- **Stirring:** Keep an eye on it and stir regularly to prevent burning
- **Garlic:** Use whole peeled cloves — they perfume the dish without overpowering
- **Soy sauce brands:** The Car brand black soy sauce and Angel brand white soy sauce are traditional Phuket products that give authentic flavor

| Minimum cook time | 1 hour |

## 🔥 Cooking Temperature

- **110-120°C** (230-250°F) — very low simmer

---

## 🍽️ Serving Suggestion

Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice. The rich, glossy sauce should coat each piece of pork.
