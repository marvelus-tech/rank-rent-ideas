=== UNBUNDLING FRAMEWORK — Concept Guide ===

FRAMEWORK: The Great Unbundling

CORE IDEA:
Big platforms (Craigslist, Reddit, Facebook, Indeed, Zillow) bundle 100s of features/categories into one site. Pick ONE category. Build a tool that's 10x better for that specific niche.

EXAMPLES FROM HISTORY:
- Craigslist housing → Zillow ($10B company)
- Craigslist jobs → Indeed, ZipRecruiter
- Craigslist services → TaskRabbit, Thumbtack
- Facebook Marketplace → Depop, Poshmark
- Reddit fitness communities → Fitness apps, Strava

HOW TO APPLY:
1. Find a big platform with 10+ categories
2. Identify which category has the most engaged/angry users
3. Build a specialized tool for just that category
4. Charge 10% of what the big platform costs but do it better

CURRENT OPPORTUNITIES:
- Unbundle Reddit: niche community tools
- Unbundle Facebook Groups: specialized group management
- Unbundle LinkedIn: niche professional networks
- Unbundle Amazon: category-specific marketplaces
- Unbundle YouTube: niche video platforms

SUCCESS CRITERIA:
- Your tool solves ONE problem 10x better than the big platform
- Users are willing to pay because the free alternative sucks for their specific need
- You can rank on SEO for "[niche] + [category]" and win
